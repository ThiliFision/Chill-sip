⚠️ Section 01 — Alcohol Awareness

The reality of alcohol

We believe in honest conversations. Here's what excessive alcohol can do — and why having a great non-alcoholic option matters more than ever.

🚗
Dangerous on the road
Alcohol slows your reaction time, blurs your vision and impairs your judgement. Drink-driving kills. In the UK alone, around 1,700 people are seriously injured in drink-drive accidents every year. If you've had a drink, hand over the keys — no exceptions.
😡
Aggression and conflict
Alcohol lowers inhibitions and can make people unpredictable and aggressive. It is a factor in over 39% of violent crimes. What starts as a fun night out can quickly turn into a situation no one wanted. The morning after, many people can't remember why they reacted the way they did.
🫀
Long-term health damage
Regular alcohol consumption damages the liver, weakens the heart, raises blood pressure and is linked to 7 types of cancer. It disrupts sleep, depletes vitamins, and accelerates ageing. The NHS advises keeping alcohol intake below 14 units per week — and less is always better.
🧠
Mental health impact
Alcohol is a depressant. While it may feel like it helps you relax, it worsens anxiety and depression over time. Many people drink to cope with stress, creating a harmful cycle. Young people are especially vulnerable — their brains are still developing until their mid-twenties.
👶
Not for young people
Alcohol has a far more severe impact on developing brains and bodies. Drinking under 18 is illegal in the UK for good reason — it can permanently affect memory, learning, and emotional regulation. Young people deserve great drinks that don't carry these risks.
💸
Social and financial cost
Alcohol misuse costs the UK economy over £21 billion a year — through healthcare, lost productivity and policing. On a personal level, it affects relationships, careers and self-confidence. Choosing alcohol-free doesn't just protect your health, it protects your future.
